## Read-out

- **Lookup: stable** 15/15 across three revisions.
- **Code: net regression 4/10 → 1/10, but informative structure.** The
  select-shape demonstration *fixed the nesting* (failures now have the
  correct `at(rotate_left(stable_unique(·), k), i)` skeleton) and the
  out-of-grammar arithmetic disappeared — both targeted failure modes
  responded to their examples. But handle-substitution *worsened*
  (9/10 vs 6/10): the rev3 additions pushed the wrong/right handle
  contrast further from the end of a longer prompt, and three
  occurrences of "given the same resource" may have weakened `resource`
  as a literal token. Under greedy decoding these small models are
  sensitive to prompt layout, not just content; example coverage works,
  but ordering/recency matters.
- **Math: 0/15 for the third consecutive revision**, byte-identical
  failure mode.

## Probe 1 — Math prompt content is irrelevant (cache-bypassed)

Three maximally different SYSTEM_MATH candidates on the same 20
construction math requests (`math_atomic`/`lookup_math`/`math_code`/
`fork_join` reference math steps, gold predecessor values):

| candidate | envelope-legal | truncated |
|---|---|---|
| rev3 (translation + boxed-mapping) | 0/20 | 11 |
| minimal blunt (5 lines, example-only) | 0/20 | 9 |
| formatter-role (no arithmetic identity) | 0/20 | 9 |

Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B-Instruct produces step-by-step LaTeX ending in
`\boxed{<computed number>}` regardless of system-prompt content. No
prompt lever remains that this evidence does not already falsify.

## Probe 2 — the endpoint model is the bottleneck, not the request

Same 20 requests, same rev3 SYSTEM_MATH, generated by the **base
Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct** (the Lookup endpoint's model):
**20/20 envelope-legal, 0 truncated**, e.g.
`<artifact>(a * b + c) % m</artifact>`,
`<artifact>((a * b) - c) / d</artifact>`.
